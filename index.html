FAQs

What's the difference between Play, Pilot & Scale, and Transform?
Play is for individual sites seeking to learn — educators get Pro accounts and access to learning resources. Pilot & Scale is for sites ready to implement, with Classroom Pro accounts, SIS integration, and custom PD. Transform is for system-wide adoption with unlimited accounts, dedicated Learning Engineer support, and full analytics.
How does student rostering work?
On the Play plan, students can access apps anonymously (unlimited) or via up to 10 student accounts per user. On Pilot & Scale and Transform plans, students are fully rostered through your SIS integration, with full analytics on usage and engagement.
Do most partners start at full pricing?
No. Most partners begin with a smaller pilot or cohort and receive 50–90% discounted pricing in Years 1–2 as adoption ramps up. Transform plans start at $6,000 regardless of enrollment tier.
What are Add-Ons?
Add-Ons are additional services that can be layered on top of any Education plan. They include professional development for staff, student AI training, custom curriculum development, custom-built AI applications, and innovation programs like design fellowships and hackathons.

FAQs

What kinds of businesses use Playlab?
Playlab works with organizations across sectors — from workforce development agencies to corporate L&D teams — to build, deploy, and scale custom AI-powered learning and productivity tools.
What's included in the Team plan?
The Team plan includes everything in Pro plus an admin dashboard with advanced analytics, access to office hours, and custom virtual professional development. It's designed for organizations that need to manage multiple users building and deploying AI tools.
When should I choose Enterprise over Team?
Enterprise is ideal for large organizations that need dedicated Learning Engineer support, custom integrations, and tailored onboarding. If your team needs hands-on guidance scaling AI across departments, Enterprise is the right fit.

*All plans include soft caps on AI usage. Published apps in paid plans currently have unlimited usage — this may change in the future.
